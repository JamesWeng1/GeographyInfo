Human Population

Population Growth & Structure

Population growth occurs when the birth rate is higher than the death rate, resulting in natural increase. If death rate exceeds birth rate, natural decrease occurs.

Population pyramids show age and sex structure and help identify dependency ratio and future population trends.

Migration & Urbanisation

Migration is the movement of people to live in another place. Rural–urban migration occurs due to push factors (poverty, lack of jobs) and pull factors (employment, education).

Urbanisation increases city populations and can lead to the growth of megacities.

Key Vocabulary – Population & Settlement

Birth Rate: Number of live births per 1000 people per year.

Death Rate: Number of deaths per 1000 people per year.

Population Density: People per km².

Dependency Ratio: Dependents compared to working population.

Settlement Hierarchy: Ranking of settlements by size and services.

CBD: Commercial and business centre of a city.

Land Use: How land is used (residential, industrial, commercial).

Rivers

Upper Course

Steep gradient, vertical erosion dominant, narrow V-shaped valleys, waterfalls formed by differential erosion.

Middle Course

Lateral erosion increases, river becomes wider and deeper, meanders form due to erosion on the outer bend and deposition on the inner bend.

Lower Course

Deposition dominates, floodplains and levees form, river may form a delta when entering the sea.

River Processes & Landforms

Erosion: Hydraulic action and abrasion.

Transportation: Traction, saltation, suspension, solution.

Deposition: Occurs when river loses energy.

Hydrographs

A hydrograph shows river discharge after rainfall. Key features include lag time, rising limb, peak discharge, and falling limb.

Case Studies

Ganges River: Flooding caused by monsoon rainfall, snowmelt, and flat floodplains.

Boscastle Flood (2004): Flash flooding caused by intense rainfall, steep valley sides, and saturated ground.

IGCSE PPQ Practice

Question: How does a delta form?

A delta forms when a river enters the sea, loses energy, and deposits sediment. Over time, layers of deposition build outwards to form a delta.
