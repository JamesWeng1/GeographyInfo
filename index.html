Human Population

Population Growth

Population growth refers to the increase in the number of people living in a country or area over time.

Vocabulary

Birth rate: Number of births per 1000 people per year.

Death rate: Number of deaths per 1000 people per year.

Migration: Movement of people from one place to another.

Rivers

Upper Course

Steep gradient, vertical erosion, narrow V-shaped valleys, waterfalls.

Middle Course

Lateral erosion increases, wider valley, meanders form.

Lower Course

Deposition dominates, floodplains, levees and deltas form.

River Vocabulary

Erosion: Wearing away of river banks and bed.

Transportation: Movement of material by the river.

Deposition: Dropping of sediment when energy decreases.

Case Study: Ganges River

The Ganges flows through India and Bangladesh. It causes flooding due to monsoon rainfall, snowmelt, and flat floodplains.

Case Study: Boscastle Flood (UK)

In 2004, heavy rainfall caused flash flooding due to steep valleys, saturated ground, and human factors.

IGCSE PPQ Practice

Question: How does a delta form?

A delta forms when a river slows down as it enters the sea, causing deposition of sediment which builds up over time.
