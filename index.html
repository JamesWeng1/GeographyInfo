Human Population

Population Change

Population change is influenced by birth rate, death rate and migration. When birth rate is higher than death rate, natural increase occurs, leading to population growth.

Population structure can be analysed using population pyramids, which show age, sex, and dependency ratio.

Settlements vary in size and function and can be ranked using the settlement hierarchy. Land use patterns change with distance from the CBD.

Rivers

River Courses

In the upper course, vertical erosion dominates due to steep gradients. In the middle course, lateral erosion forms meanders. In the lower course, deposition forms floodplains and deltas.

River Processes

Erosion, transportation and deposition shape river channels. Hydraulic action and abrasion erode the river bed and banks.

Flooding

Flooding can occur due to prolonged rainfall, short lag time on hydrographs, saturated ground and human activities such as urbanisation.

IGCSE Geography Vocabulary

Birth Rate: Number of live births per thousand people per year.

Death Rate: Number of deaths per thousand people per year.

Natural Increase: When birth rate is higher than death rate
